Efficiency & Consumption Analysis

Why does gasoline dominate in 2026?

According to our data, gasoline vehicles remain the primary choice due to the lower initial purchase cost and proven reliability in urban environments. In a market where electric infrastructure is still developing, users prefer the flexibility of modern internal combustion engines that offer reduced emissions and optimized taxes.

Premium Market Positioning

Market Leaders: Mercedes-Benz & BMW

The dominance of German brands at the top of the inventory reflects a high demand for resale value. Buyers choose these brands not only for prestige, but also for the extensive service ecosystem and parts availability. This trend indicates a market oriented toward the Premium-Used segment, where build quality justifies the long-term investment.

Estimated Depreciation Curve (Value Over Time)
Impact of the First 3 Years

Immediate Loss of Value

Our study shows that a new car loses between 20% and 30% of its value in the first year of use. This phenomenon occurs due to the transition from “new” status to “used.” Up to the 3-year mark, depreciation is accelerated, as this is when most operational leases end, flooding the market with similar models and forcing prices down.

Stability Plateau

Post-5-Year Balance

After the first 5 years, the depreciation curve flattens. The car enters a stability plateau where its value is determined more by technical condition and mileage than by age. Buyers on AutoLix often look for this “sweet spot” (between 4 and 6 years), where the major depreciation has already been absorbed by the first owner, offering the best value for money.
